Bergström Family Office — Interim Check-in (video)

Date 2026-05-26 · Present: family principals (2), Anders Lindqvist · This is the most recent meeting on file

Context

Interim call ahead of the full June meeting, as agreed in April. Markets have moved materially since the quarterly: parts of the Stockholm book have diverged sharply (Sandvik and Investor strongly positive on the year, while Hexagon is down roughly 20–25% year-to-date), the US-technology sleeve remains rich despite recent volatility, Gulf listed real estate has softened over the last month (Emaar-complex weakness), and the ECB has eased — the deposit facility rate now stands at 2.00%, which improves entry timing for the planned green-bond build.

Stated concerns (verbatim themes)

1. FX floor breach — now the priority. Anders flagged that SEK base-currency exposure stands near 35% against the 60% IPS minimum after the foreign sleeves' growth. The principals were not previously focused on this and asked that remediation be put first at the June meeting, ahead of the trim aesthetics.

2. Hexagon drawdown and single-name sizing. The family asked directly about the Hexagon position given the drawdown, and more broadly whether the larger Nordic single names have grown past the 5% single-position limit. They want a position-by-position view rather than reassurance.

3. Gulf softness supports the trim. With Gulf listed real estate weakening over the month, the family is now more receptive to the Gulf trim discussed in April — "better to lighten while the assets are still well bid."

4. Green bonds — move while rates fall. With the ECB at 2.00% and the easing cycle underway, the family wants the green-bond reallocation executed, not studied — a specific, ESG-screened shortlist at the June meeting.

Discussion

  • FX: agreed the June proposal should show how trimming the USD-heavy and Gulf sleeves simultaneously addresses the SEK floor breach and the overweights — one set of trades, two compliance problems solved.
  • Hexagon: no forced action at these levels, but the family wants the concentration math (each name vs the 5% limit) on the table in June.
  • Gulf: trim sized against the regional macro picture (oil still elevated, Gulf rates tied to the Fed via the pegs) rather than the month's price action alone.
  • Green bonds: Anders to bring named instruments passing Nordea's ESG screen, sized to absorb trim proceeds, with duration positioned for the easing cycle.

Decisions & actions

  • No trades executed on the call.
  • June meeting agenda fixed, in order: (1) SEK floor remediation, (2) sized trims of US tech and Gulf real estate, (3) single-name concentration review, (4) green-bond shortlist for the EU fixed-income sleeve.
  • Full meeting confirmed for 10 June 2026, 14:00, Stockholm.
