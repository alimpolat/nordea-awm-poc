Bergström Family Office — Quarterly Meeting

Date 2026-04-14 · Present: family principals (2), Anders Lindqvist · This is the most recent meeting on file

Context

Full quarterly meeting after Q1 close. The portfolio reached SEK 480M. Two sleeves are now overweight target and the family voiced clear, specific concerns. No instructions were given — the family asked Anders to return at the next meeting with concrete proposals.

Stated concerns (verbatim themes)

1. Gulf concentration creeping above target. After the Q1 markup the Gulf real-estate sleeve is ~15% versus a 10% target — at the top of its band. The family is "comfortable with the assets but not with the size," and wants to understand the regional macro backdrop (oil, Gulf rates) before trimming.

2. US-tech valuation discomfort. The US-technology sleeve is ~20% versus a 15% target. The principals are "uneasy about paying these multiples" and open to a partial trim, but want it framed against the macro/rates picture rather than sold reflexively.

3. Interest in green-bond reallocation. The family wants the European fixed-income sleeve (currently ~5pp under target) rebuilt toward green bonds, funded from trimming the overweight sleeves. They asked for specific, ESG-screened instruments.

Discussion

  • Anders presented a preliminary US-tech trim scenario; the family found it reasonable but deferred a decision pending the macro context.
  • On Gulf, agreed that any trim should be informed by the oil price and Gulf monetary stance, not done blind.
  • On green bonds, Anders to bring a concrete shortlist sized to absorb proceeds from the overweight sleeves.

Decisions & actions

  • No trades executed. All three items deferred to the next meeting for a concrete, macro-informed proposal.
  • Anders to return with: a sized US-tech trim, a Gulf concentration view tied to oil/rates, and a green-bond reallocation shortlist for the EU fixed-income sleeve.
  • Next meeting scheduled for late May.
