Nordea AWM · Advisory meeting notes

Bergström Family Office — Annual Review

Date 2026-01-14 · Present: family principals (2), Anders Lindqvist (lead advisor) · Next review: Q1

Context

Annual review setting posture for 2026. Portfolio closed 2025 at SEK 451M, a strong year led by Nordic industrials and the US-technology sleeve. The family is comfortable with the overall direction and reaffirmed the 10-year horizon and the moderate-growth risk profile. No change to strategic targets requested.

Discussion

  • Nordic core: principals remain happy with the industrial names (Investor, Atlas Copco, Volvo, Hexagon). Hold; no trims.
  • US technology: the sleeve had a very strong 2025 and is approaching the top of its band. Anders flagged that continued strength could push it overweight; the family is reluctant to trim winners but acknowledged the valuation risk in principle.
  • Gulf real estate: Q4 2025 results from the Dubai and Makkah holdings were ahead of expectations. Sitting near target; monitor.
  • ESG / green bonds: one principal raised — for the first time — an interest in directing future fixed-income additions toward green bonds, consistent with the family's values. Noted as a standing preference to revisit when the fixed-income sleeve is next funded.

Decisions & actions

  • No rebalancing actioned. Maintain current positioning.
  • Anders to monitor US-tech and Gulf sleeves for band breaches and bring options if either drifts overweight.
  • Green-bond preference logged in the IPS as a stated interest.
